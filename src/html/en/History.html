A Brief History of ADESJO

Origin of ADESJO

The dictatorship of Rafael Leónidas Trujillo had just ended and the Republic was enjoying its first airs of freedom. The situation in the country was still precarious. In San José de Ocoa,a group of men and women met on July 12, 1962, and formed the Development Board, later known as the Association for the Development of San José de Ocoa, Inc. (ADESJO). It was proposed that the rural communities be given the power and capacity to improve their economic and social conditions. Since that time, the institution has promoted full development through the use of the CONVITE, which is the traditional practice of people participating as volunteers in work activities carried out for a common objective, and following the slogan :
"Personal effort and mutual help".


Vision Statement

This model of full community development drives ADESJO and its community organizations to apply sustainable, innovative technologies and practice through the participation of the communities themselves, in both urban and rural areas, thereby contributing to the development of families, the promotion of a culture of democracy, and the improvement of the quality of life.


Mission Statement

ADESJO promotes the full and sustainable development of the province of San José de Ocoa and surrounding areas through the participation of its inhabitants in programs of self-determination in the social, economic, education, cultural, environmental and health related fields


Purpose of the Association

It aims to promote the economic, social and cultural development of the entire community of Ocoa, and its surrounding areas, and to raise the quality of life and level of well-being.


Philosophy

ADESJO is commited to supporting the men and women of the communities of Ocoa as they struggle to improve the conditions of the most impoverished members of society for their full development. The work of ADESJO is based on the indispensable participation of the people themselves so that they can be the social actors determining their own destiny, that is, bring self-reliance
The institution is sensitive to the motives and struggles carried out by the specific communities, and it supports them as they identify their own problems and need. Furthermore, the beneficiary populations are involved in the decision-making processes and carry out the work through the traditional practice of the CONVITE, that is, by participating in volunteer work brigades.
